Does a 1-pixel mistake in a design really matter?

A lot of designers lately have been posting screenshots of other's designs (mostly Apple UI), pointing out little imperfections and asking how it was allowed to ship.

via Brandon Walkin

Does it matter?

The fact that we need to zoom in to 1600% (or even 3200%) to see these mistakes says to me that they don't really matter. They don't stop people from using an application in the way it was intended to be used.

Did the 1-pixel impact user experience? Nope.

Do most people even notice it? Nope.

The other side

I know there is an argument to be made that great designer care about every pixel and I respect that. I try to care about every pixel in my designs, but of course I miss things and make mistakes.

The real measure of whether a design is successful should be whether or not it achieves its goal.

via Mac Tyler

I don't know of any designer who does perfect work all the time. Before attacking others for their minor mistakes, people should focus on making their own designs pixel perfect.

Every designer defines design differently but I think almost everyone agrees that design is about solving problems.

Should we strive for pixel perfection? Sure. But in the real world there are constraints, deadlines, and plenty of other pressures that impact a design. So we do the best we can within the constraints. Sometimes that means our work is less than perfect.

That's okay.
